WORLD ENVIRONMENT DAY 2018

BACKGROUND AND AIMS

World Environment Day is celebrated every year on the 5th of June.                                                    


The World Environment Day was celebrated since 1972. The celebration was meant for people to have greater consciousness and responsibility for the environments they live in. We have exploited a lot of resources and in the process we have also done a lot of damages to the environment . The environment needs our help. The celebration of the World Environment Day is an attempt to remind ourselves that we also have a responsibility towards sustaining the environment and its resources as well as keeping it clean for the present and future generations.


The aim of the World Environment Day is of two folds:

  •  To increase people’s awareness and understanding of issues affecting the environment and
  • To involve people in helping the environment


The last year theme for the World Environment Day was “Connecting People To Nature”


The theme for this year is “Beat Plastic Pollution”.

Plastic pollution is rife and it is becoming a deface and a threat to the environment. It is predicted that by 2050 there will be more plastics in the sea than the fish population! Between 60%-80% of litter in the oceans is plastic. Marshlands covered by plastic bags has less organisms and lower level oxgen.


 Our generation today is daubed as the “Throw Away Society”. There is no second thought where we dump our rubbish especially the plastic bags and plastic containers. In the streets of our cities and towns no one cares where the plastics go. In the settlements it is a very worse scenario. There is need for advocating conservation and clean environment through the ward council systems and family units.


As teachers we educate our students to go to their communities and to do the same.


The host country for this year World Environment Day is India. India is taking lead on the issues of Global Warming and Climate Change. The host country last year was Canada.

 

The activities for our school based celebration include;

poems, songs, speeches, role plays, posters, green army parade, quiz contests and Maltech Eco Flame as the Bulletin to coincide with the celebration.  

Be a green advocator…Think Globally and Act Locally!

SHRINKING WORLD... needs us

Commentary
Our world is under pressure given the size of the world’s population and the rate at which it is growing. The population at 7 billion is already having pressing impact on the environment. 
There is always growing demand for resources to have all this population survive each day. The Earth provides the sources for all the resources that people need to survive and the rate at which they are extracted is faster than the rate at which they are being replaced.

If we are not careful in educating ourselves in our actions as to how we use resources and our attitude towards taking drastic actions in conservation world wide the world environment is already shrinking and the future will be catastrophic. 

There is need for encouraging green concept in people’s attitude, technology , lifestyle, government policies, laws, manufacturing and consumption.

Imagine the Earth is a spaceship moving in a given time and space with fixed resources that could run out one day.

BEATING PLASTIC POLLUTION SPEECH By Barbie KAKEBA 10B


Plastic pollution involves increase in litters of the plastic products in the environment that harmfully affects the wildlife, wild life habitats and humans.


Plastic pollution can affect land, waterways and oceans. Living organisms, particularly marine animals can be affected through entanglement, direct ingestion of plastic waste or through exposure to chemicals within plastics that cause breakdown in biological functions. Humans are also affected by plastic pollution. Plastic reduction efforts have happened in some places in attempts to reduce plastic consumption and pollution and promote plastic recycling. Effect on the environment and distribution of plastic debris is highly variable as a result of factors such as wind, ocean currents, coastline geography , urban areas, and trade routes. Human population in certain areas also plays a large role in this.


Plastics are more likely to be found in enclosed regions such as the Caribbean. Plastics pollution potentially serves as means of distribution of organisms to remote coasts that are not their native environments. This could potentially increase the variability and dispersal of organism in specific areas that are less biologically diverse. Plastics can be used as vectors for chemical contaminants such as persistent organic pollutants and heavy metals. Plastic pollution can cause harms on soil and ocean which provide food sources to the human beings and the marine life.


How can we beat Plastic Pollution? The efforts to reduce plastic and to promote recycling has occurred. Some supermarkets charge customers for plastic bags. In some places more efficient reusable or biodegradable materials are used instead of plastics. Some societies have banned commonly used plastic items such as bottled water and plastic bags.


There are many different ways we use to reduce the plastics in our towns and places by the following:

1. People must think positively to dispose the plastics or by burning after use

2. There should be plastic recycling processing plants be built in towns where plastics are used regularly so that plastics are recycled.

3. There should be control measures be put in place at the supermarkets where plastics are heavily used 

4. Do awareness on plastic pollution and about good and bad things on plastics.

5. Government should make laws to ban plastics.


By doing the above we can beat plastic pollution. We all have a role to play by thinking globally and acting locally.


By Barbie Kakeba 10B 2018

CLOSING REMARKS BY THE TIC SOCIAL SCIENCE DEPARTMENT ON THE 2018 WORLD ENVIRONMENT DAY CELEBRATION


Thank you everyone for taking part in celebrating the 2018 World Environment Day based on the theme ”Beat Plastic Pollution”. World Environment day is a UN Environment –led global event, the single largest celebration of our environment each year and is celebrated by thousands of communities worldwide. Since it began in 1972, it has grown to become a global platform for public outreach that is widely celebrated across the globe. Most of all, World Environment Day is a day of everyone around the world to take ownership of their environment and to actively engage in the protection of the Earth.  


Here are some Plastic Pollution Facts:

  • Baby diaper took 450 years to completely decomposed. Imagine the amount that is being used everyday and how they are disposed. 
  • Plastic bags took 80 years to completely decay
  •  Plastic bottles took about 500 years to decay 
  • Every year the world uses 500 billion plastic bags
  • Each year at least 8 million tonnes of plastics end up in the seas, the equivalent of full garbage truck every minute
  • 50% of plastics we use are single use or disposable
  • We buy 1 million plastic bottles every minute
  • Plastics make up 10% of all the waste we generate 
  • By 2050 there will be more plastics in the oceans than fish


“Beat Plastic Pollution” the theme for the World Environment Day 2018, urges governments, industries, communities and individuals to come together and explore sustainable alternatives and urgently reduce the production and excessive use of single use plastic polluting our oceans, damaging marine life and threatening human health. Don’t litter with plastics and reduce its use too.


By Ms. Moish-TIC S. SCIENCE
